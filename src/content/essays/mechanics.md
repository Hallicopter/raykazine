We are moving from pages to places. The web is no longer a document viewer but a spatial environment where ideas collide.

In this essay, I explore how spatial interfaces allows us to organize information in a way that maps more naturally to our mental models. Instead of linear feeds, we have clusters of related ideas.

The desktop metaphor was a good start, but we can go further. We can create digital gardens that feel alive, where content isn't just consumed but inhabited.
